We will now check the details you have sent.

If we can confirm you’re on the shielded patients list we will send you a shielding note.

We will send your note by email to the address currently registered with your GP surgery. If we do not have an email address for you, we will send you a letter in the post.

What you need to do next

Once you have your shielding note you can give it to your employer, or the Department for Work and Pensions, if they ask to see it.

They may check the details you have provided using the Check a shielding note service.

If you have not received your note

If you have not received your shielding note by email after 1 day check your junk mail folder.

If there’s no email in your junk mail folder, wait for a letter in the post – this should take 3 days.

If you have not received a letter after 3 days speak to your GP surgery.

You can speak to the GP surgery receptionist to explain the situation – they will decide if you need to speak to a GP.

Important:

Check the details your GP surgery has on their system for you are up to date.

Sign up to the NHS App

If you need medical advice you can also get it by signing up to the NHS App.

Owned and run by the NHS, you can use the NHS App to:

  • get advice about coronavirus
  • order repeat prescriptions
  • book appointments
  • check your symptoms
